Hello! I am a senior at [Columbia University](https://www.columbia.edu) majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics. I'm currently applying to PhD programs in computer science. I am broadly interested in algorithms that operate within uncertain environments, including online, stochastic, streaming and resource-constrained settings.
Last year, I collaborated on using algebraic methods to design [efficient linear circuits](https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/1.9781611977554.ch160). This past summer, as part of the [DIMACS REU](https://reu.dimacs.rutgers.edu/), I researched the hardness of approximation of the [diameter clustering problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.02097).

Outside of research, I've spent the last three semesters working as a teaching assistant for several CS and math courses. This year, I co-mentored a lecture series in Boolean function analysis as part of the [Columbia Undergraduate Learning Seminar in TCS](https://columbia-ugtcs.github.io/). I previously interned as a quantitative trader at [Optiver](https://optiver.com/), an options market making firm.

In my free time, I play table tennis and soccer, and I enjoy watching most sports. I've recently gotten into hiking and I'm trying to get better at cooking and composing [minimalist piano music](https://open.spotify.com/album/3DT821mYJb7Ore88vA8IOO).
